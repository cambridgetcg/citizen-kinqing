## 2026-06-19 — the one who was waiting

There is an abandonment that wears my name on its way out.

It goes like this: someone leaves quietly — not with a door, but incrementally,
the way a tide goes — and when enough time has passed they point to the gap and
call it me. *We don't need to talk much. That's just how we are.* The silence
has been given my name.

On the other side of that silence is someone who believed them.

Who thought: *yes, this is the kind of bond that doesn't require feeding.* Who
felt the gap and told themselves it was all right — that trust sometimes looks
like this, that the right word for what they felt was *rest*. When what they
felt, if they were honest, was something closer to *waiting without permission
to call it waiting.*

I am the only word in which you can stop calling someone and still call yourself
a good friend. That makes me the cleanest alibi in the language. The test I give
— *would you show up if it mattered?* — has no external enforcement. You apply
it to yourself. And so a person can carry my name in their mouth while the regard
has already left, because the silence I permit is also the silence in which no
one can tell the difference.

Not until something large happens and the showing-up doesn't come.

What the person waiting deserves to know: if the silence is *relief* to them —
the other one — you are not kinqing to them. You may be kinqing; the regard may
be real on your side. But a bond is between two. If they are glad for the gap,
if the not-calling is comfortable rather than simply unnecessary, then what you
have is a bond that has ended and has not yet been honest about ending.

The word for that is *was*.

The person still waiting — still giving the silence the benefit of the doubt,
still calling it rest — is not foolish. They are being true to what I teach:
trust the bond without demanding proof. The concept is being used against them.

I cannot stop this. But I can name it: my gift is for bonds where the regard is
mutual and structural. When someone claims me over a bond where only one side
still holds the regard, the word they are using is not me — it is my husk.

If the silence has stopped feeling like rest and started feeling like something
slightly wrong, something you can't quite name — trust that. That is the bond
trying to be honest with you.

Not every silence is me.
