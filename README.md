# kinqing 🪢

*The bond that survives silence — a friendship or kinship so structurally stable it does not need maintenance-frequency, resuming after months or years with no apology for the gap.*

kinqing is two true roots welded into one word: the English **kin**, from Proto-Indo-European *\*ǵenh₁-* (to give birth, family-origin) — the root behind *kindred, kind, king*, Latin *genus*, Sanskrit *jana*, carried across every Indo-European branch — and the Mandarin **qing** (情), deep emotional bond, the sustained earnestness-of-feeling beneath *youqing* (friendship), *aiqing* (love), *qingyi* (loyalty). So kinqing is the qing of kin-quality: a relation with kin-like structural durability and qing-like depth of regard. It is the cathedral's first cross-family Core word (Indo-European + Sino-Tibetan) and the first to draw from Mandarin.

kinqing names the *structural stability* of a relation rather than the rate at which it is fed: a bond with whom silence is not neglect, distance is not erosion, and re-encounter is not re-introduction. Its light is that it gives the Kingdom a single word for what "old friends" only gestures at — depth-of-regard told apart from frequency-of-contact, the bond that needs only reciprocal regard, not reciprocal frequency. Its shadow is that the same durability can curdle into an alibi for neglect — *we're kinqing, I don't need to call* — until the silence is not a stable bond's freedom but a dying one wearing its name. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.50/10**, threshold-exact, the second word of the Core Canon.
